
# Task 2 - Unemployment Analysis with Python

## Objective
Analyze unemployment rate data, clean and explore it with Python, investigate the impact of COVID-19 on unemployment rates, identify key patterns or seasonal trends, and present insights that could inform economic or social policies.

## Methodology

1. **Data Cleaning** – converted dates, checked for missing values, sorted chronologically
2. **Trend Analysis** – visualized unemployment over time, both nationally and by region
3. **COVID-19 Impact Analysis** – split the timeline into Pre-COVID, Peak (Mar-May 2020), and Recovery periods to quantify the shock
4. **Urban vs Rural Comparison** – compared how the two area types were affected differently
5. **Labor Participation Tracking** – monitored alongside unemployment rate for a fuller labor market picture
6. **Policy Insights** – translated findings into actionable recommendations

## Key Findings
- Unemployment jumped from ~6.7% pre-COVID to ~22.4% during the March-May 2020 lockdown period
- Recovery brought rates down to ~9.3% by late 2020, still above pre-pandemic levels
- Bihar (rural, migrant-labor-dependent) saw the most extreme spike, exceeding 45% in April 2020
- Urban areas spiked sharply too but recovered somewhat faster
- Labor participation rate dipped during the peak, suggesting some workers exited the labor force entirely
